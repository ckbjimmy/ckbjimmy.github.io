I am Wei-Hung Weng, a first year PhD student at MIT CSAIL, and working with Peter Szolovits in the Clinical Decision-Making Group.

I'm interested in building an intelligent system for personalized clinical decision making. Specifically via experience-driven autonomous learning with knowledge representation using multimodal biomedical data. I use machine learning (deep learning), reinforcement learning, natural language processing, medical ontology and semantic approach. I'm also interested in medical AI innovation/startup and teaching.

Prior to MIT, I earned my MD at Chang Gung University, and graduated from Harvard Medical School with MMSc in biomedical informatics where I worked as a postdoc fellow with Laboratory of Computer Science in Massachusetts General Hospital. I also worked as primary care physician and pathologist at the medical center in Taiwan, and engaged in the field of biomedical image analysis as a research associate at National Taiwan University (NTU), Taiwan. I have contributed to several papers on journals and conferences concerning the fields of molecular cell biology, clinical data analysis, biomedical imaging, and biomedical informatics.
