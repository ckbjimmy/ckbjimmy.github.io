I am Wei-Hung Weng. I am currently a graduate student in biomedical informatics training program of Harvard Medical School, and a research fellow in Laboratory of Computer Science in Massachusetts General Hospital.

I'm interested in realizing personalized medicine using predictive analytics and enormous unstructured medical information. Currently I'm solving the problems of clinical domain and expert prediction and recommendation for better clinical referral using large clinical dataset, identifying potential refractory seizure patients using unstructured medical records, medical concept identification for undiagnosed diseases patients, and better clinical decision making in intensive care unit for clinicians using large ICU database. I use machine learning, deep learning, natural language processing, medical ontology and semantic approach. I'm also interested in biomedical informatics innovation/startup and teaching (currently the teaching assistant of the HMS graduate course).

I earned my M.D. degree at Chang Gung University, worked as primary care physician and pathologist and engaged in the field of biomedical image analysis as an R&D associate in National Taiwan University (NTU). With more than 3 years of research experience, I have contributed to several papers on journals and conferences concerning the fields of molecular cell biology, clinical data analysis, and biomedical imaging.
