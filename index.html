bio

I am a first year PhD student at MIT CSAIL, and advised by Professor Peter Szolovits in the Clinical Decision-Making Group. Prior to MIT, I earned my MD at Chang Gung University, and graduated from Harvard Medical School with MMSc in biomedical informatics where I worked as a postdoc fellow with Laboratory of Computer Science in Massachusetts General Hospital. I also worked as physician and pathologist at the medical center in Taiwan, and engaged in biomedical image analysis as a research associate at Ultrafast Optics lab in National Taiwan University.

My current research focuses on building an intelligent system for personalized clinical sequential decision making by deep reinforcement learning and representation learning with multimodal biomedical data.
