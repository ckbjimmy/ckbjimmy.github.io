I am a Research Scientist at Google current based in the Greater Boston Area. My current research focuses on developing machine learning learning methods to learn robust and generalizable representations for low data regime and personalization in healthcare. More broadly, our team, based in California and London, is working on advancing machine learning capabilities and evaluting innovative and impactful early research in the healthcare domain.

I received my PhD in Computer Science from Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), advised by Professor Peter Szolovits in MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, Clinical Decision Making Group. I mainly worked on learning representations for limited and heterogeneous medical data. Prior to MIT, I received my MD at Chang Gung University, and MMSc in Biomedical Informatics at Harvard Medical School. I also worked as general physician and pathologist for years.
